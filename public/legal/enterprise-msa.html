1. Background and Scope

1.1 Stagify operates an AI-powered virtual staging platform at stagify.ai, including Stagify+ features (advanced models, AI Designer, mask editing, variations, and related tools) and enterprise domain-based access for organizations.

1.2 This Agreement governs Customer's organizational purchase and use of Stagify Enterprise, under which Authorized Users with email addresses on Customer's approved domain(s) receive Stagify+ capabilities subject to the terms herein and any applicable Order Form.

1.3 This Agreement is intended for business and organizational customers. Individual consumer subscriptions to Stagify+ are governed by Stagify's consumer Terms of Service and Privacy Policy, not this Agreement, unless Customer and Stagify execute a separate written amendment.

1.4 If there is a conflict between this Agreement and an executed Order Form, the Order Form controls for that order only. If there is a conflict between this Agreement and Stagify's general website Terms of Service, this Agreement controls for Customer's Enterprise use.

2. Definitions

  • "Authorized Domain(s)" means the email domain(s) listed in the Order Form (e.g., company.com) approved for Enterprise access.
  • "Authorized Users" means Customer's employees, contractors, and agents who create Stagify accounts using email addresses on an Authorized Domain and use the Services solely for Customer's internal business purposes.
  • "Generated Output" means images and other content produced by the Services from Customer Content.
  • "Customer Content" means photographs, floor plans, prompts, files, and other materials submitted by or on behalf of Customer or Authorized Users to the Services.
  • "Generation" means one billable AI staging, mask edit, or other metered output event as defined in the Order Form or Stagify's then-current Enterprise metering policy.
  • "Order Form" means a written or electronic order, checkout confirmation, or statement of work executed by the Parties describing Authorized Domain(s), fees, and commercial terms.
  • "Services" means Stagify Enterprise access, Stagify+ feature provisioning for Authorized Users, usage reporting, and related support described in this Agreement and the Order Form.

3.3 Stagify may update, modify, or improve the Services, including underlying AI models and feature sets, provided such changes do not materially degrade the core staging functionality during a paid billing period without reasonable notice.

3.4 Customer is responsible for informing Authorized Users that use of the Services is subject to this Agreement, Stagify's Privacy Policy, and applicable Stagify policies.

3.5 Individual Stagify+ subscriptions. If an Authorized User separately purchases an individual Stagify+ subscription with their own payment method, that user's Generations will be billed under their individual subscription and will not be counted toward Customer's Enterprise usage metering, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Exhibit A — Data Processing Addendum (DPA)

This DPA forms part of the Agreement between Stagify and Customer where Stagify processes Personal Data on behalf of Customer in providing the Services.

A.1 Roles

Customer is the controller (or business, under applicable U.S. state law) of Personal Data relating to Authorized Users and content they submit. Stagify is the processor (or service provider) with respect to such data, except where Stagify acts as an independent controller for its own account administration, billing, security, and product analytics as described in the Privacy Policy.

A.2 Processing details

  • Subject matter: Provision of Stagify Enterprise and Stagify+ features
  • Duration: Term of the Agreement plus retention periods in the Privacy Policy
  • Nature and purpose: Account management, authentication, AI staging, logging, billing metering, support, security
  • Categories of data subjects: Authorized Users and individuals identifiable in Customer Content
  • Categories of Personal Data: Name, email, account identifiers, usage metadata, IP address, prompts, and images that may contain incidental personal data

A.3 Customer instructions

Stagify will process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Customer as set forth in the Agreement, Order Form, and Customer's use of the Services, unless required by law (in which case Stagify will notify Customer where permitted). Stagify will promptly inform Customer if, in Stagify's reasonable opinion, a processing instruction from Customer would infringe applicable data protection law, unless Stagify is prohibited by law from doing so.

A.4 Security, breach notification, and DPIA assistance

Stagify implements appropriate technical and organizational measures, including encryption in transit, access controls, and password hashing, as described in the Privacy Policy. In the event Stagify becomes aware of a personal data breach affecting Personal Data processed on Customer's behalf under this DPA, Stagify will notify Customer without undue delay and, where feasible, no later than 72 hours after becoming aware, providing sufficient information for Customer to meet its own breach notification obligations under applicable law. Stagify will cooperate with Customer and take reasonable commercial steps to assist in the investigation and remediation of any such breach. Stagify will also provide reasonable assistance to Customer in conducting data protection impact assessments (DPIAs) required by applicable data protection law, to the extent such assistance relates to processing under this DPA and information is available to Stagify.

A.5 Subprocessors

Customer authorizes Stagify to engage the subprocessors listed above, including Google (Gemini AI, OAuth), OpenAI, Stripe, Resend, and cloud hosting providers. Stagify will impose data-protection obligations on subprocessors by contract or standard terms substantially equivalent to those in this DPA. Stagify will notify Customer at least 30 days before engaging a new subprocessor that will process Customer's Personal Data. Customer may object in writing within 14 days of such notice on reasonable data protection grounds. If the Parties cannot resolve a reasonable objection, either Party may terminate the affected Services on 30 days' written notice without liability for the termination itself.

A.6 International transfers

Personal Data may be processed in the United States and other countries. Where required, Stagify will rely on appropriate transfer mechanisms such as the Standard Contractual Clauses issued under European Commission Decision 2021/914 (Module Two: controller to processor), the UK International Data Transfer Addendum (IDTA) issued by the ICO, or other equivalent safeguards as applicable to the jurisdiction of the data subject.

A.7 Assistance and rights requests

Stagify will reasonably assist Customer in responding to data-subject requests and regulatory inquiries relating to processing under this DPA, considering the nature of processing and information available to Stagify.

A.8 Deletion

Upon termination, Stagify will delete or return Personal Data processed on Customer's behalf within 30 days, except where retention is required by law or permitted under the Privacy Policy (e.g., billing records, security logs). Upon request, Stagify will provide written confirmation that deletion has been completed.

A.9 Audit

Upon reasonable written request no more than once per year, Stagify will provide information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA, or allow for a third-party audit under confidentiality, subject to Stagify's security requirements and except where prohibited by law.

A.10 Confidentiality of processing personnel

Stagify will ensure that persons it authorizes to process Customer's Personal Data are subject to appropriate obligations of confidentiality, whether by contract, professional obligation, or statutory duty.
