Masking Studio & AI Designer: The Two Features That Set Stagify Apart

By Stagify.ai · July 10, 2026 · 6 min read

One-click AI staging is great when you want a furnished photo in seconds. But real listings aren't always that simple. Sometimes you need to change only part of a photo, place a specific piece of furniture, or keep refining a room until it's exactly right. That's what Stagify's two Stagify+ power features are built for.

One-click staging is the floor, not the ceiling

Most AI staging tools stop at a single button: upload, pick a style, take what you get. It's a great starting point — and it's how Stagify's free staging works too. But "take what you get" breaks down the moment you want control: a photo where only the corner needs furniture, a room where you love the rug but not the couch, or a space that needs three rounds of tweaks before it's listing-ready. Stagify adds two tools for exactly those moments.

The Masking Studio: pixel-level precision

The Masking Studio lets you decide exactly which parts of a photo can change — and leaves everything else untouched. Here's how it works:

  • Upload a photo of your room.
  • Highlight where each piece of furniture should go — paint it, drag a rectangle, or use the Magic tool to select an object or area with a single click. Use one color per area.
  • Give every area its own furniture photo, a short prompt, or both — or switch an area to Remove object to clear out clutter.
  • Click Apply Edit. Every area is staged at the same time, and only the highlighted spots can change. The rest of your photo stays pixel-perfect.
  • Not happy with an area? Try another version to generate more options and flip between them.

Why that matters:

  • Surgical control. Furnish an empty corner, swap a single chair, or clear clutter — without regenerating the whole room.
  • Keep what you love. A great view, real furniture, or a feature wall stays exactly as photographed.
  • Place your own furniture. Attach a specific sofa or bed image to an area, and it goes right where you drew it.
  • Honest by design. Because masking never touches the room's structure — walls, windows, flooring — your photo stays a true representation of the space, which makes it easy to disclose on the MLS.

The AI Designer: conversational, agentic staging

Where the Masking Studio is about precision, the AI Designer is about open-ended creativity. Instead of picking a preset and hoping, you have a conversation:

  • Upload a room and simply tell the AI what you want — stage it, redesign it, change the style, or swap a single piece.
  • It responds, and you keep going: "make it warmer," "add a reading nook," "try a mid-century look." The AI iterates with you until the room looks listing-ready — no starting over.
  • It can even turn CAD floor-plan PDFs into photorealistic 3D room renders, so a blueprint becomes a room buyers can picture.

Why that matters:

  • Refine, don't restart. Complex, specific looks come from a back-and-forth, not a single lucky click.
  • No design skills needed. Describe what you want in plain language and let the AI make the decisions.
  • From blueprint to room. Turn a floor plan into a rendered, furnished space for pre-construction and renovation listings.

Precision + creativity = results one-click can't match

Most tools do one thing: they stage a photo, once. Stagify gives you the two capabilities they're missing — exact control with the Masking Studio and open-ended iteration with the AI Designer.
